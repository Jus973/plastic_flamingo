Section 3: Understanding Long Dwell Signals

In user experience analytics, long dwell time can indicate several things:

  • Engagement: The user is genuinely interested in the content
  • Confusion: The user is struggling to understand what to do next
  • Information Overload: Too much content requiring extended reading time
  • Distraction: The user may have switched tabs or been interrupted

The long_dwell signal is triggered when a user spends more than 10 seconds on a page. Combined with other signals, this can help identify potential UX issues.

For example, a long dwell time combined with multiple clicks or form attempts might indicate confusion, while a long dwell time on a content-heavy page with smooth scrolling behavior might indicate genuine engagement.
